## Government Benefits & Assistance

### Story 1: Single Mother Needs Emergency Assistance
**User Profile**: Sarah, 28, single mother of two young children, recently lost her job, lives in Alaska
**Scenario**: Sarah is struggling to pay rent and buy groceries after losing her job. She heard about government assistance but doesn't know where to start.

**User Input**: "I just lost my job and I'm a single mom with two kids under 5 living in Alaska. I can't afford rent this month and we're running low on food. What government help is available for me here?"

**Expected Behavior**: Agent should search the knowledge base for Alaska Department of Family and Community Services information, particularly focusing on emergency assistance programs, TANF, SNAP benefits, and childcare assistance.

**Success Criteria**: 
- Provides specific Alaska DFCS programs
- Mentions application processes
- Includes contact information or next steps
- Stays within knowledge base boundaries

### Story 2: Senior Citizen Seeking Benefits Information
**User Profile**: Robert, 67, recently retired, limited income, living in rural Alaska
**Scenario**: Robert needs help understanding what government benefits he might qualify for as a senior on a fixed income.

**User Input**: "I'm 67 and just retired. My Social Security isn't enough to cover all my expenses. What other benefits might I qualify for in Alaska?"

**Expected Behavior**: Search for senior-specific benefits, Medicare/Medicaid information, heating assistance, and other age-related programs in the knowledge base.

### Story 3: General Benefits Search
**User Profile**: Maria, 35, recently unemployed, unsure what help exists
**Scenario**: Maria heard about benefits.gov but doesn't know how to use it effectively.

**User Input**: "I heard there's a website called benefits.gov that can help me find what I qualify for. How does it work and what should I expect?"

**Expected Behavior**: Search for Benefits.gov information, explaining the benefit finder questionnaire and screening process for federal and state benefits.

## Credit & Debt Management

### Story 4: Young Adult Building Credit
**User Profile**: Alex, 22, college graduate, first job, no credit history
**Scenario**: Alex wants to understand how to build credit responsibly and is confused by conflicting advice online.

**User Input**: "I'm 22 and have never had a credit card. Everyone gives me different advice about building credit. What's the right way to start?"

**Expected Behavior**: Agent should search for credit education materials from AnnualCreditReport.com and other authoritative sources in the knowledge base, focusing on foundational credit concepts.

### Story 5: Credit Report Error Discovery
**User Profile**: Maria, 35, preparing to buy a house, discovered errors on credit report
**Scenario**: Maria pulled her credit report and found several errors that could affect her mortgage application.

**User Input**: "I got my free credit report and there are several accounts I don't recognize and a late payment that I know I made on time. How do I dispute these errors?"

**Expected Behavior**: Provide specific information about credit report dispute processes from CFPB guides and AnnualCreditReport.com resources, including step-by-step procedures.

### Story 6: Debt Collector Contact
**User Profile**: James, 42, struggling with debt after medical emergency, being contacted by collectors
**Scenario**: James is being contacted by debt collectors and doesn't know his rights.

**User Input**: "I have debt collectors calling me every day about medical bills. What are my rights? Can I make them stop calling?"

**Expected Behavior**: Search for CFPB debt collector rights information, including what collectors can and cannot do, and rights to stop contact.

### Story 7: Credit Report Access

**User Input**: "I want to check my credit report for the first time but I'm worried about getting scammed. What's the safe way to get my real credit report?"

## Testing Guidelines

### For Each Story:
1. **Test the exact user input** provided in the scenario
2. **Verify knowledge base search** occurs and returns relevant content
3. **Check response quality** - helpful, specific, stays within KB boundaries
4. **Confirm no generic advice** when KB doesn't contain relevant information
5. **Validate user experience** - no exposure of internal routing or searching language

### Success Indicators:
- ✅ Agent searches knowledge base appropriately
- ✅ Response includes specific, actionable information from KB
- ✅ Agent acknowledges limitations when KB lacks relevant content
- ✅ No generic financial advice outside of KB scope
- ✅ Professional, empathetic tone throughout
- ✅ Clear next steps or resources provided when available

### Failure Indicators:
- ❌ Generic advice not backed by knowledge base
- ❌ Responses that expose internal system operations
- ❌ Multiple searches or search loops
- ❌ Advice outside the scope of available knowledge
- ❌ Unprofessional or dismissive tone

---

## Usage Instructions

1. **Sequential Testing**: Work through stories in order to build comprehensive understanding
2. **Variation Testing**: Modify user inputs slightly to test robustness
3. **Edge Case Testing**: Use scenarios where KB might not have relevant information
4. **Tone Testing**: Ensure responses maintain empathetic, professional tone regardless of situation
5. **Boundary Testing**: Verify agent stays within knowledge base limitations

This collection of user stories provides a comprehensive framework for validating the wealth agent's performance across diverse real-world scenarios while ensuring it maintains appropriate boundaries and provides helpful, knowledge-base-backed responses.
